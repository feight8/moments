# Light Theme Approach (Clean & Inviting)

A light theme is highly readable and great for informational pages, leaderboards, or community forums.

- **Primary Background:** Use `#fffffa` (Off-white). This provides a clean canvas that reduces eye strain compared to pure stark white.
- **Secondary Background (Cards, sidebars):** Use `#d4f5f5` (Light Cyan). This subtly separates content areas without breaking the bright, airy feel.
- **Primary Body Text:** Use `#554348` (Dark Plum/Charcoal). This offers excellent, ADA-compliant contrast against the off-white and light cyan backgrounds.
- **Headings (H1, H2, H3):** Use `#255957` (Deep Teal). This creates a clear visual hierarchy and breaks up the text with a strong, branded color.

# Dark Theme Approach (Immersive & Gaming-Focused)

Dark modes are incredibly popular in the gaming space and can make your accents "pop."

- **Primary Background:** Use `#255957` (Deep Teal) or `#554348` (Dark Plum).
- **Secondary Background:** If using the deep teal as your main background, use the dark plum for panels, footers, or navigation bars.
- **Primary Text & Headings:** Use `#fffffa` (Off-white). This provides striking, high-contrast readability against both of your dark backgrounds.

---

# Buttons and Call-to-Action (CTA) Styles

Your buttons need to stand out while remaining completely legible for visually impaired users.

- **Primary Action Button (Play Now, Sign Up):** Use your bright `#ffad69` (Peach/Orange) for the background.
  - **ADA Critical Rule:** Do not use white text on this button; it will fail contrast checks. Instead, use your dark plum (`#554348`) or deep teal (`#255957`) for the button text to guarantee it is bold and readable.
- **Secondary Button (Learn More, About):** Use the deep teal (`#255957`) as the background with the off-white (`#fffffa`) for the text.
- **Hover States:** When a user hovers over a primary orange button, you can invert it: make the background dark plum and the text orange.

---

# Accents and Highlights

- **Links in Text:** Use the deep teal (`#255957`) for links in your body text, and add a bold weight or an underline to ensure color isn't the only visual indicator of a link (a key ADA requirement).
- **Borders & Active States:** Use the orange (`#ffad69`) as a top border for active navigation tabs or as a subtle outline around form inputs when they are clicked.
